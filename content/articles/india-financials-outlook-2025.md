## Executive Summary

Indian private sector banks are entering a phase of sustained ROE expansion driven by improving liability franchises, digital distribution, and benign credit costs. We remain constructive on well-capitalized lenders with proven deposit gathering capabilities.

## Structural Tailwinds

Credit penetration in India remains low relative to GDP, providing a long runway for loan growth. The shift from public sector banks to private players has accelerated post-pandemic, with private banks gaining share in both retail and SME segments.

### Deposit Franchise Quality

Banks with granular retail deposits trade at a premium for good reason: funding cost stability through rate cycles supports NIM resilience. We favor institutions demonstrating consistent CASA ratio improvement.

## Key Risks

- Regulatory changes to unsecured lending norms
- Competition from fintech-led credit models
- Macro slowdown impacting asset quality in unsecured books

## Conclusion

We maintain a positive bias on top-tier private banks and highlight HDFC Bank as a core holding in our India financials coverage.
